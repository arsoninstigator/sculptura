non-academic interests

if you've ever met me, you know i’m powered by utter delusion, aka the absolutely unrealistic expectations of how much can be done in a single lifetime and the deep, unshakable urge to take every criticism as a personal challenge to prove you wrong.

there are just... too many things i want to do. too many skills i want to learn, hobbies i want to try, books i want to read, cities to explore, people to love, languages to speak, projects to build and memories to make. i want to live twelve different lives at the same time and be good at all of them. i know that’s not how the world works but the little girl in me is still holding a candle for that dream life where i somehow do it all.
.

creative hobbies + arts

my instagram bio says “professional procraftinator,” and honestly? it’s never been more accurate. i love the arts — all of them. i’ve been dancing professionally since i was five, singing since seven, and picking up instruments like they’re collectable figurines. i do graphic design, illustration, ui/ux, and 3d design (the non-parametric, art-driven kind). i enoy songwriting and music production and am working on my own debut album, because of course i am. i also ran a full-blown jewellery design + making business — think cad modelling, resin printing + wax carving, lost wax metal casting, polishing etc. paired with beadwork, wire wrapping... you name it. i’m a diy-till-i-die kind of person, i'm always picking up hobbies for no reason at all, like one day it's cyanotypes and screenprinting, the next it's lockpicking and lampworking. and amidst this chaos, i’m apparently now deep in a forum thread from 2009 about how to restore a typewriter.
.
